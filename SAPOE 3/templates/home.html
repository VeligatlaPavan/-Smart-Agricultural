Parameters Analyzed by OptiCrop

Nitrogen (N)

Crucial for vegetative crop growth, leaf development, and chlorophyll production which triggers photosynthesis.

Phosphorus (P)

Promotes healthy root structures, flowering density, and seed creation. Critical during early growth cycles.

Potassium (K)

Enhances disease resistance, controls stomata opening (water retention), and improves product quality and yield.

Temperature

Defines seasonal suitability. Affects the rate of photosynthesis, transpiration, and general metabolic activities.

Humidity

Regulates transpiration rates. High humidity impacts fungal development, while dry air increases water requirements.

pH Level

Determines nutrient solubility and soil microbial activity. Most crops thrive in neutral to slightly acidic conditions (5.5 - 7.0).

Rainfall

The primary source of moisture. Excess water can cause root oxygen depletion, while low rainfall triggers drought stress.
